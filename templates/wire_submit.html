Wire Transfer — Pre-Release Screening

Simulates a payments-ops user submitting a correspondent-banking wire for release. The ordering customer and beneficiary names are screened against sanctions, PEP, and internal watchlist data before the transaction posts. A match blocks the wire immediately and raises an alert in the Open Queue — it does not wait for the next batch engine run.

This only applies to CORRESPONDENT accounts (Category 3) — the category where payment messages carry distinct ordering/beneficiary names that must be screened independently of the account holder. View screening log →

How this works

Clean wire
All names clear screening → transaction posts immediately with status CLEARED.
Matched wire
Any name hits a sanctions, PEP, or watchlist entry → transaction posts with status BLOCKED and an alert fires in the Open Queue right now — no waiting for the next engine batch run.
Fuzzy match
Near-identical names (transliteration variants, phonetic matches) are caught in addition to exact matches. A confidence level is shown on the result and on the alert.
Batch-loaded transactions
Historical transactions loaded by the pipeline are marked CLEARED by default — they never went through live pre-release screening, since they represent already-occurred history.
