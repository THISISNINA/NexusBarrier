How NexusBarrier works

Three stages between a raw transaction and a defensible regulatory decision.

Step 1

Automated data ingestion

Transactional data streams are ingested continuously and normalized into your workspace's isolated ledger. Accounts, counterparties, jurisdictions, and channels arrive ready to screen, with no manual uploads or lookups.

Step 2

Risk-scored alert generation

A weighted risk score, layered over pattern-based detection scenarios, flags high-risk activity such as structuring, PEP and sanctions exposure, and rapid movement. A severity-graded alert is raised the moment a threshold is breached, tagged with the exact rule version that fired it.

Step 3

Seamless team collaboration

Analysts triage and investigate. The MLRO signs off on escalations and SAR decisions, and workspace admins govern access. Every hand-off is audited, and every closure carries a documented narrative.

The life of an alert

Every flag follows the same auditable path. Expand each stage to see what happens there.

A detection scenario's threshold is breached and an alert lands in the open queue, unclaimed. It arrives complete with the transaction context, severity grade, and the exact rule version that fired it.

A first-line analyst claims the alert, reviews account history and related activity, and documents findings. Simple items can be closed directly, and anything needing compliance judgment moves up.

The MLRO owns the escalated queue: confirm and close, or send the alert back to the analyst with an audit comment for further investigation. The flow is reviewable in both directions.

Every closure, whether a SAR filing or no action, requires an investigative narrative, and SAR paths demand a valid goAML reference. Nothing terminates without a defensible paper trail.
