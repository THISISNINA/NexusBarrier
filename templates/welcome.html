Welcome to NexusBarrier AML Monitoring

A quick overview before you get started — what this system watches for, how an alert moves through review, and what each role is responsible for.

What this system does

NexusBarrier continuously screens transaction activity against 10 detection scenarios — pattern-based rules covering structuring, high-risk jurisdictions, PEP exposure, sanctions screening, dormant-account reactivation, rapid fund movement, and more. When activity matches a scenario's threshold, an alert is raised automatically and enters the review queue below.

This is a local, air-gapped instance — no data leaves this machine, and the sanctions list / scenario thresholds are illustrative, not a live regulatory feed. See the scenario reference at the bottom of this page for exact trigger logic.

How an alert moves through the system

OPEN

Alert raised automatically. Sitting in the queue, unclaimed.

→
UNDER_REVIEW

An analyst (or MLRO) claims it and begins investigating.

→
ESCALATED

Analyst escalates for second-line sign-off, or closes it directly if no escalation is needed.

→
CLOSED_SAR CLOSED_NO_ACTION

Terminal state. A SAR was filed (with a goAML reference), or the alert was closed with no action — either way, a documented narrative is required.

An MLRO can also send an ESCALATED alert back to UNDER_REVIEW if it needs more investigation before sign-off — the flow isn't strictly one-directional.

{% if current_role == 'MLRO' %}

Your role: MLRO (second-line review)

As MLRO, you provide second-line oversight on alerts an analyst has already investigated and escalated. Your queues:

  • Dashboard — system-wide status counts and the oldest alert still awaiting action.
  • All Alerts — every alert in the system, any status.
  • Open Queue (All) — OPEN and ESCALATED alerts together, so you're never blind to what's still moving through the analyst tier. You can claim a fresh OPEN alert yourself if needed.
  • Escalated Queue — the full inbox of everything awaiting sign-off, from any analyst.
  • My Reviews — alerts you've personally opened, whether you claimed them yourself or they were escalated to you.

From an alert's detail page you can close it (SAR or no-action, with a required narrative), return it to the analyst for more work (with a required note), or create or link a case to group related alerts on the same account.

{% else %}

Your role: L1 Analyst (first-line review)

As an analyst, you're the first line of investigation — triaging newly raised alerts, documenting findings, and deciding whether to close an alert directly or escalate it for MLRO sign-off. Your queues:

  • Dashboard — system-wide status counts and the oldest alert still awaiting action.
  • All Alerts — every alert in the system, any status.
  • Open Queue — unclaimed OPEN alerts awaiting investigation.
  • My Reviews — alerts currently assigned to you (UNDER_REVIEW).

From an alert's detail page you can claim it, close it (SAR or no-action, with a required narrative), escalate it to the MLRO, or create or link a case to group related alerts on the same account.
